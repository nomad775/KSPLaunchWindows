Orbit Equations

Uniform circular motion

$$v= \sqrt{\frac{GM}{2}} = \sqrt{\frac{\mu}{r^2}} $$

From balance of forces

$$\frac{2GMm}{(R+r)^2} = m\omega^2r \approx GM = \omega^3r^3$$

\(\omega^2r^3\) is constant. Period can be found by

$$\omega = \frac{2\pi}{P} \Rightarrow P^2 = \frac{4\pi^2a^3}{G(M+m)} \approx P^2 = \frac{4\pi^2a^3}{GM}$$

$$P = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{a^3}{\mu}}$$

At Ap and Pe flight angle is zero so

$$r_pv_p = r_av_a$$

at other locations $$v\sin{\gamma} = \omega r^2$$ where \(\gamma\) is the zeineth angle

From Conservation of Engergy

Velcoity and radius at Ap and Pe

\[ v_p = \sqrt{\frac{2\mu r_a}{r_p(r_a+r_p)}}\]

\[ v_a = \sqrt{\frac{2\mu r_p}{r_a(r_a+r_p)}}\]

\[ r_a = \frac{r_p}{(\frac{2\mu}{r_p v_p^2}-1)} \]

\[ r_p = \frac{r_a}{(\frac{2\mu}{r_a v_a^2}-1)}\]

Eccentric Orbits

Eccencticity can found from \(r_a\) and \(r_p\) or it also be fround directly by

\[ e = \frac{r_pv^2}{\mu}-1\]

\[r_p = a(1-e)\]

\[r_a = a(1+e)\]

Taking \(\phi\), the flight angle as \(90-\gamma\)

\[e=\sqrt{(\frac{rv^2}{\mu} - 1) \cos^2{\phi} + sin^2{\phi}}\]

True Anomaly

\[\tan{\nu} - \frac{ \frac{rv^2}{\mu} \cos{\phi}\sin{\phi}}{\frac{rv^2}{\mu}cos^2{\phi}-1}\]

\[a = \frac{1}{\frac{2}{r}-\frac{v^2}{\mu}}\]
